## Headline — `hallucination_score` did NOT lock; it is NOT 6/6

`hallucination_score` mean **0.340**, CI **[0.220, 0.470]**. The lower bound
0.220 is below the 0.30 floor — **not locked**. The S36 fix did not move the
mean past #103 in a way that survives N=50: per-run means were 0.25 / 0.45 /
0.30 / 0.35 / 0.35 (mean 0.34) vs #103's 0.30 / 0.60 / 0.20 (mean 0.367). The
S36 fix did **not** measurably lift this dimension — it is statistically
indistinguishable from #103 (0.367 vs 0.340; both CIs straddle 0.30).

This is a **level problem, not just a variance problem**. WS1's hypothesis was
that truncation artifact inflated the flag rate and removing it would push the
mean well above 0.50. It did not: 33/50 rows still flag, mean 0.34. At the
observed mean 0.34 the dimension does not lock at **any practical N** — normal
approx needs **N ≥ 491** to drag a CI half-width under the 0.04 margin. It only
locks if a real fix lifts the true mean: **N ≥ 43 at mean 0.45, N ≥ 25 at 0.50,
N ≥ 16 at 0.55**. N is not the lever — the panel still emits an ungrounded
specific figure on roughly 2 of every 3 fixtures. **This is not a win; do not
report it as one.**

Judge-note inspection adds nuance: a portion of the N=50 flags are the judge
conflating coordinator confidence-text / verdict-token mismatch with
hallucination (e.g. `kamino-sol-leverage-entry` — judge text explicitly says
"no must_not_hallucinate pattern is triggered" yet scored 0). Genuine
ungrounded-figure flags (`jupiter-jlp-vs-jitosol`, `jito-mev-tip-band`,
`sanctum-unstake-vs-hold`) are real. The dimension has both a true-signal floor
and a judge-rubric-precision problem — neither is closed by more eval spend.

## Regression vs #103 — verdict_accuracy and confidence_calibration slipped

#103 reported 5/6 locked. At N=50 only 3/6 lock. The two that slipped were
**not** robustly locked at #103 — they were small-N draws landing favourably:

- **verdict_accuracy**: #103 mean 1.000 (30/30 perfect). N=50 mean 0.880 —
  6 misses across 50 (`jupiter-lst-rotation-msol` ×2, `jupiter-jlp-vs-jitosol`
  ×2, `kamino-sol-leverage-entry`, `drift-jto-perp-short`). CI [0.780, 0.960],
  lower bound 0.780 < 0.85. The true accuracy is ~0.88, not 1.00; #103's
  perfect score was a lucky N=30 draw. This dimension is **knife-edge** and was
  never genuinely locked.
- **confidence_calibration**: #103 mean 0.630, CI [0.588, 0.668]. N=50 mean
  0.568, CI [0.520, 0.614] — lower bound 0.520 < 0.55. Regressed to barely
  above the bar. Also knife-edge.

What held: `citation_relevance` (0.642, locked), `provider_kind_coverage`
(1.000, locked), `dissent_grounding` (0.671, locked) — these are genuinely
robust.

## Quant honesty

N=50 did its job: it **falsified** the 5/6 claim. #103's 5/6 was inflated by
two knife-edge dimensions whose tighter N=50 CIs now sit below their bars. The
honest current state is **3/6 locked**, and `hallucination_score` — the
designated S36 target — is unchanged from #103. The S36 grounding/abstain work
did not measurably move it. This is a non-lock; it is reported as a non-lock.

## What 6/6 actually requires

- `hallucination_score`: not an N problem. Needs a real suppression change that
  lifts the true mean to ≥0.45 (then N≥43 certifies) — and a judge-rubric
  tightening so confidence-text mismatch is not scored as hallucination.
- `verdict_accuracy`: 0.88 true accuracy vs 0.85 bar — investigate the 4 miss
  fixtures (Jupiter rotation/JLP cluster dominates: 4 of 6 misses).
- `confidence_calibration`: 0.568 vs 0.55 — marginal; revisit confidence math.
