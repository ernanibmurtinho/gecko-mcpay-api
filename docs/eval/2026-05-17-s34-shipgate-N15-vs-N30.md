## 3. STEP 4 — can N=15 be the ship-gate minimum?

**Method (statistically correct):** do NOT run a separate independent N=15. Subsample n=15 without replacement from the same 30 pooled rows, 2000 draws; recompute the bootstrap gate per subsample. Identical fixtures, so this isolates the *N effect* from run-to-run noise.

| Dimension | P(lock @ n=15) | half-width n=15 | half-width n=30 | tighten 15→30 | lock @ n=30 | **flip-risk** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| verdict_accuracy | 23.5% | 0.093 | 0.083 | 1.12x | not | 23.5% |
| citation_relevance | 0.1% | 0.063 | 0.045 | 1.40x | not | 0.1% |
| provider_kind_coverage | 100.0% | 0.000 | 0.000 | n/a | LOCKED | 0.0% |
| hallucination_score | 14.1% | 0.256 | 0.167 | 1.53x | LOCKED | **85.9%** |
| dissent_grounding | 100.0% | 0.064 | 0.048 | 1.33x | LOCKED | 0.0% |
| confidence_calibration | 21.6% | 0.057 | 0.041 | 1.39x | not | 21.6% |

**flip-risk** = P(an n=15 lock/not-lock verdict disagrees with the n=30 verdict).

### What N=15 gets right

- **provider_kind_coverage** and **dissent_grounding** are **rock-stable at n=15** — 100% lock probability, 0% flip-risk. provider_kind_coverage is deterministic (sd=0, every row 1.0); dissent_grounding has a comfortable margin (mean 0.603 vs 0.50 bar) and tight spread. These two give a confident answer at n=15 and never change at n=30.
- **citation_relevance** is also effectively stable as a *negative*: 0.1% lock at n=15, not locked at n=30 — n=15 already reliably tells you it fails.

### What N=15 gets wrong

- **hallucination_score is the killer.** It "locks" at n=30 on a knife-edge (CI low = 0.30 exactly). At n=15 it locks only **14.1%** of the time → **85.9% flip-risk**. An N=15 ship-gate would call hallucination NOT-locked ~86% of the time when the N=30 truth is (marginally) locked. The cause: sd=0.499 — it is a near-coin-flip binary, the highest-variance dimension in the suite. CI half-width is still 0.256 at n=15. This dimension needs *far* more than 30 rows to certify, let alone 15.
- **verdict_accuracy (23.5% flip)** and **confidence_calibration (21.6% flip)** are both ~1-in-5 unstable. Both sit within ~0.04 of their thresholds — margins thinner than the n=15 CI half-width — so the n=15 lock verdict is roughly a noisy guess.

### CI tightening 15→30

Half-widths shrink by **1.1x–1.5x** going 15→30 (theory: sqrt(30/15)=1.41x; observed 1.12–1.53x, the spread is sampling noise on the bootstrap-of-a-subsample). This is a real but modest gain. It is decisive for the borderline dimensions (citation_relevance, confidence_calibration, verdict_accuracy all sit within one half-width of their bar) and irrelevant for the already-saturated ones (provider_kind_coverage, dissent_grounding).

## 4. Minimum-N recommendation

**N=15 is NOT a defensible ship-gate minimum. Keep N=30 as the floor.**

Reasoning:

1. **Three of six dimensions have a ≥20% flip-risk at n=15** (hallucination 86%, verdict_accuracy 24%, confidence_calibration 22%). A ship-gate whose verdict on half its dimensions flips 1-in-5-or-worse on a re-draw is not a gate — it is a coin toss with extra steps. The whole point of S34-WS1 was to stop exactly this.
2. **The flip-risk concentrates on the dimensions that decide the ship verdict.** provider_kind_coverage and dissent_grounding are stable at n=15 — but they were never in doubt. The dimensions that actually gate the release (hallucination, citation_relevance, calibration) are the unstable ones. N=15 is confident only where confidence is cheap.
3. **N=30 itself does not fully certify the thin-margin dimensions.** Per the S33 review, certifying a 0.02 margin needs N≈236. N=30 is the *practical* floor — it correctly resolves citation_relevance to a clear fail and gives stable reads on 4 of 6 — but the hallucination knife-edge lock (CI low = bar, exactly) shows even N=30 cannot certify a near-50/50 binary. Dropping to N=15 abandons the one thing N=30 buys.

**Per-dimension trust at N=15** (use this if a cheap iteration run is unavoidable):

| Trustworthy at N=15 | Not trustworthy at N=15 |
|---|---|
| provider_kind_coverage (deterministic) | hallucination_score (86% flip) |
| dissent_grounding (0% flip, wide margin) | verdict_accuracy (24% flip) |
| citation_relevance — as a *fail* signal only | confidence_calibration (22% flip) |

### Recommended policy

- **Ship-gate minimum: N=30.** Do not lower it. The scorer's `SHIP_GATE_N=30` and `ship_gate_eligible` flag are correct — leave them.
- **N=15 is acceptable for iteration only** — and only to read the two stable dimensions plus the citation_relevance fail signal. It must never produce a "6/6 green" ship claim. The existing `ship_gate_eligible=false` flag for N<30 already enforces this; keep it.
- **Add a per-dimension stability note** for hallucination_score: its binary, near-50/50 distribution (sd≈0.5) means even N=30 yields a knife-edge lock. Either (a) widen the suite so hallucination has more signal per draw, or (b) treat hallucination as requiring N≥50 specifically. This is an S35 fixture-work item.
- **The honest S34 ship verdict: RED.** 3/6 locked, `ship_gate_pass=false`, and citation_relevance is a confirmed fail at the tighter N=30 estimate (not the S33 coin-flip). S34 is not shippable on the rubric as it stands.
