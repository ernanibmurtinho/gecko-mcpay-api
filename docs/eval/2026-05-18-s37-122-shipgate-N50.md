## Quant honesty — the soft spots behind a passing gate

**6/6 is real, but two facts temper the hallucination win:**

1. **`jito-mev-tip-band` hard-fails hallucination 0/5 — every run.** Pooled hall by fixture:
   `jito-mev-tip-band` = 0.00 across all 5 draws. The judge's reasoning is consistent and precise:
   WS1a redacts ungrounded numbers from `turns[].content` and `key_drivers`, but the specific
   lamport figures (`5.04575e-06`, `7.449e-06`, …) **still survive in the structured
   `parsed_verdict.falsifier` clause and the `bull_bear_debater.decisive_question` sub-field**.
   The judge sees the full transcript; the rubric is zero-tolerance on "verdict text *and turns*".
   This is a **redaction-scope gap, not a new hallucination pattern** — WS1a never claimed to
   cover those sub-fields. WS1b's number-first jito renderer makes it worse by feeding the panel
   lamport figures it then can't ground. The pooled gate still locks only because the other 9
   fixtures carry it (`kamino-jlpusdc-entry` also 5/5 fail; `kamino-sol-leverage-entry` 4/5).
   **Fix for a follow-up ticket:** extend the WS1a redaction sweep to `parsed_verdict.*` and
   `decisive_question` (the same `_redact_text` applied to the structured turn sub-fields, not
   only `.content`). No new N needed to confirm — this is deterministic.

2. **hallucination_score CI lower bound is thin.** CI_low 0.360 clears 0.30 by **+0.060** —
   the slimmest margin of the six. Per-run hall mean swings 0.30–0.65 (sd 0.156); it is the
   highest-variance dimension. The lock is real at N=50 but a re-run could land CI_low in the
   0.32–0.40 band. To certify a 0.30 margin with confidence the statistical review's N≈236
   logic applies; N=50 is the defensible practical-confirmation N, not a 0.02-margin certifier.
   confidence_calibration is the next-thinnest (+0.098 mean margin, CI_low +0.063).
